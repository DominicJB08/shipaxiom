# Ship Axiom Offer Research

Date: 2026-06-15

## Recommendation

Keep the business idea, but do not sell default on-site AI PC installation. The stronger offer is:

> Private workflow automation, local-first where it matters.

Ship the first workflow remotely in a private workspace or in the customer's own cloud/server account. Use local models on existing customer hardware when practical. Quote hardware, travel, and on-site appliance builds separately after the workflow proves value.

## Pricing

The original `$299 / $2,500 / $750/mo` ladder is too low for a serious privacy-first AI ops offer. It risks attracting low-intent buyers and underfunding the real work: discovery, security boundaries, workflow design, QA, handover, and support.

Recommended public packages:

| Package | Price | Purpose |
|---|---:|---|
| AI Workflow Audit | `$2,500` | Workflow map, risk review, automation opportunities, pilot scope. |
| Ship Sprint | `$9,500` | One production workflow, private remote deployment, documentation, 30-day handover support. |
| Ops Care | `$1,250/mo` | Monitoring, fixes, tuning, reporting, and small improvements. |

Regulated workflows should be quoted higher after the audit:

| Package | Price |
|---|---:|
| Regulated Audit | `$4,500` |
| Ship Sprint Plus | `$15,000-$18,500` |
| Regulated Pilot | `$22,500+` |
| Managed AI Ops | `$2,500/mo+` |

## Delivery Model

Default path:

1. Remote audit and workflow map.
2. Build one workflow in a private project workspace.
3. Use policy routing: local/private for sensitive tasks, approved cloud/API/GPU only for non-sensitive or heavy tasks.
4. Deploy into customer-owned infrastructure where practical.
5. Offer local hardware only as a funded add-on.

This keeps capital low and avoids becoming a hardware reseller before demand is proven.
